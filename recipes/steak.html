smoked steak not grilled

steak bathed in smoke marinated for 12 hours

This Bourbon-Compound Butter Smoken steak is a similar idea as the reverse-sear method. We are using our trager then moving to a cast iron skillet on stove.

Ingredients

  • Ny strip
  • stubbs steak rub
  • 1 stick salted butter
  • 1 green onion top
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1.5 - 2 tbsp Wellers Kentucky bourbon

Steps to cook

  1. Set the butter out on your counter at room temperature until it softens
  2. Add the butter, green onion, parsley, paprika and Bourbon to a bowl and mix well
  3. Move the butter mixture to plastic wrap. Roll up in the plastic wrap then place in the refrigerator until the butter hardens.
  4. Set the Masterbuilt Smoker to 225 degrees F and add pecan wood chips.
  5. Place the NY Strip steaks in the smoker.
  6. For medium rare, remove the steak when it reaches 129 to 132 degrees F. This will take a bit less than 1 hour depending on your actual smoker temperature and outside temperature.
  7. Move the steaks to high heat on the grill and sear both sides. This only takes about 2 minutes per side.
  8. Remove the steaks from the grill and cut a couple of slices of the Bourbon Butter and place on the steaks.
  9. Let the steaks rest for 10 minutes, then serve.
